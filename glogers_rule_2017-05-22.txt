Exploring Gloger’s eco-geographic rule
Arjun Amar & Petra Sumasgutner
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Gloger’s eco-geographic rule states that animals should be darker in more humid or wetter environments (Gloger 1833). Evidence has recently been presented that it also applies to plants (Koski & Ashman, 2015). In this essay, I would like to you discuss the origin of this eco-geographic rule, the empirical support for this rule and the likely mechanisms that are proposed to explain it (e.g. Burtt & Ichida, 2004; Tate et al. 2016).ReferencesGloger, C.
